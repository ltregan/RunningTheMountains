My Running Injury part #1: Achilles Tendinitis

November 12th 2015

In this serie, I'll describe running injuries I went through. I'm not giving advice to anybody, but just describe what I went through.

What happened ?

I was on my local 10k trail race, running at a pretty good speed of 3:55 / km. In the last 3km, there are a couple of short downhill sections of about 100m. The grade is steep enough to propel you forward, but not so steep you need to break - needless to say you go at full speed on it. I was wearing minimalist shoes (New Balance MT10) I had trained with at moderate speed, but never really pushed it with them.

During the downhill, I suddenly felt very strong pain in my Achille tendon. It was as if the foot was loose without much support from the tendon. I finished the last 3km running, without slowing down in any way.

Following that, I could barely walk for a week and did not run for 3 weeks.

History

Even before my running days, I always had high sensitivity of my Achille tendons. For instance pushing on my foot while skating would make it painful for a couple of days.

Since I started running, I had chronic pain. Waking up in the morning, tendons would feel pretty stiff and painful until I walk a little. Wearing 6mm drop shoes, I would also feel a mild pain after a run. Adding 2mm inserts (so 6+2=8mm drop) would greatly alleviate it.

Amazingly, swimming for 40min would totally remove any of the pain for a couple of hours. I am not sure if that is due to the swimming, or the fact that I swam in river and the cold immersion was helping.

I had experience with 0mm drop shoes, wearing them to work every day. I had also made a small dozen of 5-10k runs with them, but all at moderate speed.

Treatment and Recovery

I did not see any inflammation or bruise in my foot, I thus concluded the tendon was not broken and - good or bad - I did not consult a doctor.

I did a few exercises to make the calf muscles stronger and stretch things out, following the Stanish protocol, followed by icing after each session. It is documented here.

Aftermath

3 weeks after the injury, and without any running in between, I attended the Tuscany 100k ultra. Everything went good during the race, and could complete it in a good time.

Strangely, running 12h at moderate speed seemed to have helped heal the injury.

About 2 years later, I significantly increased my mileage from 100k/month to 300k/month. In the process, the chronic pain disappeared and I don't feel anything waking up in the morning now.

Learnings

  1. Speed is dangerous ! You may have the fitness, but it does not mean the rest of your body will follow.
  2. Current wisdom is to use heavy shoes for training and lightweight for racing. I think doing the opposite is much safer ! I did use more cushioned and higher shoes the year after at the same race, won 3min on the 45min compared to the year before and had not issue whatsoever.
  3. I should probably have addressed my chronic pain earlier
  4. Achille tendinis is a good example where active recovery is pretty well accepted in the field; Stanish's protocol rules !
  5. Minimalist shoes are fun to run with, but use them with reason.
